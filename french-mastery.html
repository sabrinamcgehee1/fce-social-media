Before Everything Else

Your Polyglot Core System

This is not a standard language course. This is the system used by the world's top polyglots — rebuilt around your life as a busy mom with twins. Everything in this guide connects back to these four daily pillars.

⚠️

Honest Truth: C1 in 9 months from zero with limited time is extremely ambitious

Not impossible — but it requires elite efficiency, zero wasted effort, and the right techniques. The realistic outcome with discipline is functional B2 / approaching C1 by December 2026. For content creation, this is more than enough — and genuinely extraordinary. Reframe your goal: not "C1 or failure" but "expressive French that inspires your followers." The polyglot system below is your fastest path there.

The Foundation of Everything

The Polyglot Core 4

Do these every single day. Everything else — grammar, writing, content — is a bonus on top of this engine. Miss a day? Come back to Core 4.

🃏

1. SRS (Anki)

Review words right before you forget them. Use sentences, not isolated words — this is how polyglots do it.

5–10 min · Memory
🔊

2. Shadowing

Listen and speak simultaneously with a native speaker. Copy rhythm, intonation, emotion. This is your gym for fluency.

5–10 min · Fluency
⛏️

3. Sentence Mining

Collect real sentences from content you consume. Save them to Anki. You learn vocabulary, grammar, and phrasing all at once.

5 min · Real Language
🗣️

4. Speaking

Produce French every day. Talk to yourself, an AI, or record yourself. Even 3 sentences counts on chaos days.

5–10 min · Output

TOTAL MINIMUM: 20–35 minutes · split throughout your day while the twins eat, nap, or play

1

The No-Zero-Days Rule

You will have bad days. Twins will be sick. Work will explode. You'll be exhausted. On those days, you do the Minimum Viable Day — nothing less. Skipping entirely resets the habit loop. 5 minutes counts. A zero day does not.

No zero days. Ever.
2

Output From Day 1

You must speak and write in French starting from Week 1 — even if it's broken, even if it's just to yourself. Confidence under pressure only comes from output under pressure. You will never feel ready. That feeling never goes away. Speak anyway.

Speak before you're ready.
3

Your Learning IS Your Content

You are not a student who happens to make content. You are a creator who learns publicly. Every mistake, every breakthrough, every confused face is content. The accountability of your followers will push you further than any study schedule ever could.

Document, don't just study.

☁️ Minimum Viable Day

For twins-in-chaos / exhausted / no time days

  • 5 min Anki review only (no new cards)
  • 5 min Shadow 3 sentences from yesterday's audio
  • 5 min Say 5 French sentences out loud — narrate what you're doing

SURVIVAL MODE (2 min): Listen to 1 French sentence. Repeat it out loud. Done.

beats zero."

✓ Already Yours

  • Nasal vowels (um, em, an → un, en, on)
  • Grammatical gender on all nouns
  • Subjunctive mood — you use it daily in PT
  • Romance verb conjugation structure
  • Thousands of near-identical cognates
  • Latin-root vocabulary intuition

⚠ Watch Out For

  • The guttural French R — nothing like PT R
  • Silent final consonants (liaison rules)
  • The ü sound (u/ou distinction)
  • "Faux amis" — false cognates (actuel = current, not actual)
  • Silent -e and rhythm differences in speech
  • Spelling vs. pronunciation gaps

Non-Negotiable Structure

Your Weekly
Output System

Input (listening, reading) builds understanding. Output (speaking, writing) builds fluency. Without this weekly structure, you will plateau at B1 and never break through to C1.

Polyglot Weekly Flow

🗣 Speaking Day

1 day/week focused only on output. Role-play scenarios (Alter Ego), 20-min AI conversation, record yourself, review your Error Bank out loud.

Deliberate Practice Day

🎯 Deep Practice Day

1 day/week focused ONLY on your current weakest area — pronunciation, listening speed, verb tense, or writing precision. Deliberate practice, not general review.

Recovery Day

☁️ Light / Survival Day

1 day/week: MVD only. Anki + shadow 3 sentences + say something out loud. Protect your streak without burning yourself out.

Every day (non-negotiable)

🔊 Daily Shadowing + Speaking — 15 min

Shadow 3–5 sentences from your current resource (repeat simultaneously with the audio). Then narrate your day in French — describe your twins, your tasks, your thoughts. Micro-sessions count: shower, dishes, walk.

Try: "Je prépare le dîner. Les jumeaux jouent. Aujourd'hui j'ai appris le subjonctif."

2–3× per week

💬 French-Only Conversations

Structured French sessions with your husband. No English fallback. Use body language, point at things, use "comment dit-on…?" but stay in French. 10 minutes is enough. The rule is: no switching.

Rule: French only. Mistakes welcome. English is not an option.

The Feedback Loop

Your Error Bank

This is the system that separates people who reach C1 from people who stay at B1. Without tracking your errors, you will repeat the same mistakes for months — and never know why you're not improving.

Every time you make a mistake — in speaking, writing, or correction tools — log it. Once a week, spend 15 minutes reading through it out loud. This is your personalised grammar textbook.

How to log

  • Use a physical notebook OR a Google Doc — pick one, stick to it
  • Write the wrong version first, then the correct version
  • Add a category: grammar / vocabulary / expression / pronunciation
  • Review every 3–4 days — read each entry out loud, say the correct version 3×
  • When you stop making that mistake: cross it out with a green pen ✓

The jump from B2 → C1 is almost entirely about eliminating your personal error patterns — not learning new grammar. Your Error Bank is how you do that.

For Portuguese Speakers

Pronunciation Guide

You already own the hardest sounds in French. Here's exactly what's easy and what needs deliberate practice.

✓ Easy for You (Portuguese Advantage)

ã õ

Nasal vowels (un, on, en/an)

You use these daily in PT: irmã, bom, tempo. Minor adjustment only.

le/la

Article pronunciation rhythm

Romance language rhythm is in your ears already. Much easier than for English speakers.

-tion

French -tion endings (say "syon")

PT -ção gives you this naturally. Nation, situation, organisation all work.

-eur

Open-mid vowel (professeur)

Similar to PT open e sounds. Practice: cœur, sœur, heure.

⚠ Needs Practice (Key Differences)

r

The French guttural R

Unlike any PT R. Produced at the back of throat, like gargling gently. Practice: rouge, river, Paris. Listen on Forvo.

u

The French "ü" sound (/y/)

Lips rounded for "ou" but say "i". No equivalent in PT. Practice: tu, rue, vu. Listen on Forvo daily.

h

Silent H (always silent in French)

Unlike PT, never aspirated. L'homme, l'hôpital. "H aspiré" is still silent but blocks liaison.

liaison

Liaison (word linking in speech)

"Vous avez" → "vouz-avey". Words blend in speech. The best way to learn: shadowing podcasts.

The Psychology

The Polyglot Mindset

The method matters less than the mindset. These are the rules that separate the people who reach fluency from those who give up at month 3 — drawn from the world's best language learners.

8 Rules That Make or Break You

1

Consistency beats intensity. 25 minutes every day destroys 3 hours once a week. Miss one day — fine. Miss three in a row — you're in danger. The habit is the method. Micro-sessions throughout your day count as much as one big block.

2

Speak before you're ready. You will never feel ready. The discomfort of speaking badly is the price of learning to speak well. Start Week 1, even with five words. Every polyglot you admire started sounding terrible.

3

Make French your environment, not your subject. Phone in French. 70% of YouTube in French. Background audio daily. French dinner with your husband 2× a week. Think in French while cooking. You don't need Paris. You need immersion at home.

4

Trust the plateau. Fluency doesn't feel gradual — it feels like long frustrating plateaus followed by sudden leaps. The leap always comes. When you feel stuck, you are actually learning. Every polyglot goes through this. You are not the exception.

5

Aim for 70%, advance at 70%. Don't wait for 100% comprehension before moving on. If you understand 70% of what you hear, you're ready for the next level. Perfection is the enemy of fluency.

6

Remove friction, don't add complexity. You don't need a perfect system. You need a survival-proof system. On the worst days: 5 minutes of Anki + shadow 3 sentences + say something out loud. That's it. That's enough.

7

You are a French speaker, not a French learner. Stop saying "I'm learning French." Say "Je parle français — I'm at B1 and growing." The identity precedes the fluency. Polyglots adopt the new language identity early. You should too.

8

Use better energy, not more time. You don't have more time — you have twins. But you can use your existing time better. Shadowing during chores. Anki during snack time. Self-talk in the shower. Better use of your energy beats a bigger study schedule every time.

Technique 01 · Daily Non-Negotiable

Shadowing

Listen and speak simultaneously with a native speaker · Used by Luca Lampariello

Fluency · Pronunciation

What It Is

You listen and speak at the same time as a native speaker — like a shadow of their voice. You copy not just the words but the rhythm, intonation, speed, and emotion. Think of it as a gym for your mouth and ears combined.

WHY IT WORKS

Trains your brain to process French in real time. Builds automatic speaking (fluency), improves accent, and develops listening comprehension simultaneously.

How To Do It

1

Play your audio (Coffee Break French, podcast, video)

2

Repeat immediately without pausing — speak simultaneously with the speaker

3

Copy pronunciation, rhythm, intonation, emotion — not just the words

4

Do this for 5–10 minutes every single day, even on chaos days

French Example

AUDIO SAYS:

"Je suis en train de préparer le dîner pour les enfants."

YOU (AT THE SAME TIME):

"Je suis en train de préparer le dîner pour les enfants."

Don't stop when you miss a word. Keep going. The flow is what you're training.

For you: while doing dishes, feeding twins, walking — earphones in, shadow out loud.

Technique 02 · Memory Engine

Spaced Repetition (SRS)

Review words right before you forget them · Used by Anki, SuperMemo

Vocabulary · Long-term Memory

What It Is

The algorithm reviews each card at the optimal moment — just before your brain would forget it. Each successful recall pushes the next review further into the future. After enough repetitions, a word moves into permanent long-term memory.

WHY IT WORKS

Moves knowledge into long-term memory with the least amount of study time. Extremely efficient — 10 minutes of SRS daily beats an hour of passive re-reading.

The Polyglot Upgrade

❌ BASIC (avoid)

apprendre = to learn

✓ POLYGLOT (sentence cards)

"j'essaie d'apprendre le français avec les jumeaux"

You learn: vocabulary + grammar + natural phrasing — all at once.

Rules for Busy Moms

→Do Anki first thing, before anything else — even before coffee
→If your review pile exceeds 80 cards — add zero new cards that day
→From Month 4: pull new cards from content you're actually consuming
→Start: French Core 2000 deck on ankiweb.net — free

Technique 03 · Real Language Acquisition

Sentence Mining

Collect real sentences from content you consume · Popular in immersion communities

Vocabulary · Natural Phrasing

What It Is

Instead of studying vocabulary from a preset list, you collect sentences that appear naturally in content you are already consuming — shows, podcasts, articles. You then save them to Anki and review them in context.

WHY IT WORKS

Builds a mental "phrase bank" of real, natural language. Context-based learning is far more memorable than isolated words. You learn vocabulary, grammar, and natural phrasing simultaneously.

How To Do It

1

While watching or listening, notice a phrase you don't know or want to use

2

Pause and write down the full sentence (not just the word)

3

Add it to Anki as a sentence card — highlight the new word/phrase

4

Mine 1–3 sentences per session — quality over quantity

French Example

FROM A PODCAST YOU HEARD:

"Ça ne m'étonne pas du tout."

(That doesn't surprise me at all.)

YOU LEARN IN ONE CARD:

Vocabulary (étonner) + negation + colloquial rhythm + natural usage — all from one sentence you actually heard.

Technique 04 · Pattern-Based Learning

Palteca Method / Chunking

Learn language through patterns, chunks, and repetition — not grammar rules

Grammar · Speaking Speed

What It Is

Instead of memorizing grammar rules, you memorize and drill useful sentence patterns — then swap words inside them. You learn language the way children do: through repetition of structures, not analytical study of rules.

WHY IT WORKS

Mimics how children acquire language. Reduces overthinking. Builds ready-to-use building blocks that come out automatically when you speak — no grammar analysis in real time.

French Pattern Examples

PATTERN: "j'ai envie de ___"

j'ai envie de dormir · j'ai envie de café · j'ai envie de partir

PATTERN: "je suis en train de ___"

je suis en train de manger · je suis en train de travailler · je suis en train d'apprendre

PATTERN: "ça fait ___ que ___"

ça fait un an que j'apprends · ça fait longtemps qu'on ne s'est pas vu

Variation Repetition Drill

Take one verb and drill it with variations — same structure, different inputs. This automates grammar without ever studying a rule chart.

DRILL: manger

je mange du pain
je mange avec toi
je ne mange pas
j'ai mangé ce matin
je veux manger maintenant

Technique 05 · Natural Acquisition

Comprehensible Input (i+1)

Consume content just above your level · Stephen Krashen

Listening · Reading · Acquisition

What It Is

Consume content that is slightly above your current level — but still mostly understandable. Linguist Stephen Krashen calls this "i+1": your current level (i) plus one step up. You understand ~70–80% and figure out the rest from context.

WHY IT WORKS

Triggers natural language acquisition — the same process children use. Less stressful than studying, and produces more durable retention. The key is "understandable challenge," not confusion or boredom.

Your i+1 Ladder

A1

Extra en Français, Peppa Pig en Français (YouTube)

A2

Coffee Break French, Language Transfer, LingQ A2 stories

B1

InnerFrench podcast, Le Monde Junior, French vlogs

B2

France Inter radio, RFI, French Netflix, Le Monde

C1

France Culture, native novels, unscripted French video

The 70% Rule

70%

If you understand 70% of what you hear or read, you are at the right level. Move up. Don't wait for 100%.

Below 50% → too hard, step down. Above 85% → too easy, step up. 70% is the sweet spot for acquisition.

Technique 06 · Precision Training

Bidirectional Translation

Translate PT → FR, then FR → PT · Used by Luca Lampariello

Writing · Grammar · Precision

What It Is

Write or translate sentences from PT to FR. Then — a day or two later — translate them back from FR to PT without looking at the original. Where the reconstructed PT differs from your original, you found a gap.

WHY IT WORKS

Forces precision at every level — vocabulary, structure, prepositions. Reveals the exact gaps that speaking and passive listening never expose. Powerfully underrated technique.

Step-by-Step

1

Write 5 sentences from your daily life in Portuguese

2

Translate them to French as best you can

3

Wait 24–48 hours — cover the PT original

4

Translate your French back into Portuguese — then compare to the original

5

Any difference = add to your Error Bank

French Example

PT ORIGINAL:

"Vou viajar amanhã de manhã."

YOUR FR (Day 1):

"Je vais voyager demain matin."

RECONSTRUCT PT (Day 2):

"Vou viajar amanhã de manhã." ✓

Where it doesn't match → that's your gap. That's what goes in the Error Bank.

Technique 07 · Psychology of Speaking

Alter Ego / Role-Playing

Create a new identity in the language · Benny Lewis

Speaking · Confidence

What It Is

Create a French persona with a name, a personality, and a life story. When you speak French, you become her — not a Brazilian mom learning French, but a French speaker expressing herself. This psychological shift removes the fear of embarrassment and judgment.

WHY IT WORKS

Mistakes belong to the persona, not to you. This removes the ego barrier. Benny Lewis used this to reach conversational fluency in 12+ languages, starting each one from zero.

Build Your French Persona

GIVE HER A NAME

Pick a French name — Sophie, Claire, Léa. This is who speaks French. Not you studying it.

GIVE HER A STORY

She's a mom of twins living in Paris. She creates content. She speaks with confidence.

BECOME HER WHEN SPEAKING

When you open your mouth in French, switch. Mistakes are hers, not yours. She's bold.

Your French Mantra

"Je suis une maman française.
Je parle, je crée, j'apprends.
Je ne suis pas parfaite —
je suis courageuse."

Say this in French every morning. Identity precedes fluency.

Technique 08 · Maximum Efficiency

Deliberate Practice

Focused practice on your weaknesses only · Anders Ericsson

All Skills · Maximum ROI

What It Is

One session per week dedicated 100% to your single biggest weakness. Not general review — surgical focus on one specific gap. Researcher Anders Ericsson found this is how experts in every field improve fastest.

WHY IT WORKS

General practice maintains your level. Deliberate practice on weaknesses raises it. 30 minutes of focused weakness-work gives more improvement than 2 hours of comfortable review.

Monthly Weakness Targets

M1–2

Pronunciation — the French R, the ü sound. Shadow daily for this only.

M3–4

Verb tense confusion (passé composé vs imparfait) — drill only this.

M5–6

Listening speed — train with fast native audio until it becomes comfortable.

M7–9

Error Bank review — your personal recurring mistakes are your target.

How To Run a Session

1

Identify your one biggest weakness right now

2

Spend 30 min on that one thing only — nothing else

3

Get feedback — record yourself, use AI correction, or compare to a native example

4

Repeat next week until that weakness is no longer your biggest one

Technique 09 · Listening Mastery

Deep Listening Loop

Listen to the same audio multiple times with different goals each pass

Listening · Comprehension

What It Is

Instead of listening to an episode once and moving on, you listen to the same audio 3 times over 3 days, but with a different focus each time. Each pass extracts different value from the same material.

WHY IT WORKS

Each pass trains a different skill. You extract 3× the value from the same resource — crucial when you have limited time and can't constantly find new content.

The Three Passes

DAY 1 → GENERAL UNDERSTANDING

Listen without stopping. What's the general topic? What do you understand? Don't look anything up yet. Tolerance of ambiguity is a skill.

DAY 2 → CATCH NEW WORDS

Listen again. Pause on 3–5 new expressions. Look them up. Mine the best ones for your Anki deck as sentence cards.

DAY 3 → SHADOW IT

Listen and speak simultaneously. You now know the content — use that security to focus entirely on pronunciation, rhythm, and flow.

Perfect Resources for This

M1–3

Coffee Break French — structured, clear, repeatable

M4–6

InnerFrench — conversational, near-native, natural

M7–9

France Inter radio clips, Arte documentaries

For you: 1 episode = 3 days of content. Massive ROI for a busy schedule.
