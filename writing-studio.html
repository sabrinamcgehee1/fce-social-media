Your Master Story Framework
Every piece of content you create — video, Substack, carousel, story — follows this core structure. Memorize this. It's your creative spine.
Before you write anything, answer these 3 questions:
1. Which account? @fluencyconnect (authority + method) · @sabrina (personal brand + growth) · @michael+sabrina (couple + culture)
2. What's the job? Attract (40%) · Nurture (25%) · Position (20%) · Convert (15%)
3. What's the source? A life moment · A Kinnu card · A Substack phrase · A Deepstash idea · An essay video insight
1
Moment
A real, specific situation from your life. Not vague — cinematic. Where were you? What time was it? What were you doing? The more specific, the more universal it becomes.
Weak: "Being a mom is hard."
Strong: "It was 2:17am. One baby was crying. The other had just fallen asleep. My English homework was open on the kitchen counter."
2
Emotion
What you actually felt — not what you think you should have felt. Name the ugly emotions: guilt, shame, jealousy, frustration, inadequacy. These are what connect. Your audience feels seen when you say the thing they've been thinking but couldn't articulate.
Example: "I felt like a fraud. A woman who teaches English but couldn't even finish a paragraph because her babies needed her."
3
Shift
This is your signature move — the moment something changed in your understanding. Not a generic epiphany. A real cognitive shift that came from experience, not theory. This is where you position your method, your brand, your worldview.
Example: "Then I realized — I wasn't failing at English. I was failing at forgiving myself for not being perfect at everything simultaneously."
4
Message
Turn your shift into a lesson your audience can apply. One idea. One mindset change. One practical step. End with a power line — the sentence people screenshot and share. Then your CTA, matched to the content's job (attract = engagement, convert = WhatsApp/link).
Power lines:
"You don't need more time — you need better focus."
"You don't lack English. You lack confidence using it."
"The best time to start was yesterday. The second best time is right now, with your babies watching."

Hook Library
6 hook formulas you can rotate. Click any to copy the template, then adapt it to your story. Never open the same way twice.
Contrarian / Hot Take
"Everyone says [common belief]. But here's the truth…"
"Everyone says you need to study 2 hours a day to learn English. That's why most Brazilians never become fluent."
Confession / Vulnerability
"I need to tell you something I've never shared…"
"I'm an English teacher and I still make grammar mistakes. Here's why that matters."
Identity Call-Out
"If you're a [specific person], this is for you."
"If you're a Brazilian mom who wants to learn English but has zero time — stop scrolling."
Cliffhanger / Story Open
"[Specific scene]. I had no idea what would happen next."
"It was 11pm. Both twins were finally asleep. I opened my laptop and started crying."
Data / Number
"[Surprising number] and here's why it matters."
"Brazilians study English for an average of 8 years. And most still can't hold a conversation."
Nobody Talks About
"Nobody talks about [hidden truth about your niche]."
"Nobody talks about the guilt of choosing work over your babies — even for 30 minutes."
Story Starters (grab and go)
LIFE: "No one talks about what it's really like having twins…" · "Today I almost gave up…" · "When Michael and I disagree about how to raise the girls…"
ENGLISH: "This is why Brazilians study for years and still can't speak…" · "I used to translate everything in my head until…" · "The mistake I hear from every single student…"
GROWTH: "I thought I was disciplined… until I became a mom" · "The habit that changed how I learn forever" · "I run an English school with 7h/week for social media. Here's my system."

Source Capture
What triggered this content? Write the phrase, idea, or moment that sparked it. This connects your learning pipeline to your content.
Ask yourself: "What did I learn/see/feel that I can't stop thinking about?" — that tension is your content.
1
Hook
Pattern interrupt + identity. You have 3 seconds. Call your exact audience and make them stop scrolling.
Formulas: Contrarian ("Everyone says X, but…") · Confession ("I need to tell you…") · Identity ("If you're a Brazilian mom…") · Cliffhanger ("It was 2am. Both twins were crying.") · Nobody talks about ("Nobody talks about…")
2
Micro-Story
A specific, visual scene. Keep it simple and real. What happened? Where? When? Add sensory details — what did you see, hear, feel physically?
Think cinematic: "It was 2:17am. One baby was crying. The other had just fallen asleep. My English homework was open on the kitchen counter, untouched."
3
Struggle
Show both the external struggle (what was happening) and the internal struggle (what you were thinking/feeling). The internal part is what builds loyal audience.
Name the ugly emotion: guilt, shame, frustration, inadequacy, confusion, fear. "I felt like I was failing at everything — motherhood, work, even my English."
4
Shift
Your signature move. What did you change? What did you realize? This is where your brand shines — Fluency Connect method, mindset, lived experience.
Position your method: This isn't generic advice. This comes from YOUR experience as a Brazilian bilingual mom running a school. What did YOUR life teach you that books can't?
5
Lesson + Power Line
Turn your story into value: 1 idea, 1 mindset shift, or 1 practical tip. Then write a power line — the sentence people screenshot and share.
Power line formula: "You don't need [what they think] — you need [what actually works]." Make it quotable.
6
CTA
Match your CTA to the content job you selected.
Attract: "Comenta 👇" / "Você já passou por isso?"
Nurture: "Salva esse vídeo" / "Manda pra alguém que precisa"
Position: "Link na bio" / "Segue pra mais conteúdo assim"
Convert: "Manda ESTRATÉGIA no WhatsApp" / "Link na bio — é de graça"
Substack Newsletter Draft
Your deep format. This is where you become unforgettable. Follow the 10 steps — each one builds on the last.
Angle
Source
0
Source Capture
What triggered this newsletter? The phrase, the card, the moment.
1
Title
Identity + curiosity. Make them click because they see themselves in the title.
Examples: "What Having Twins Taught Me About Discipline" · "Why Most Brazilian Women Never Become Fluent" · "The Day I Realized I Was Wasting My Time"
2
Cinematic Opening
Start like a movie. Drop them into a moment — no preamble, no context. Time, place, sensation.
Example: "It was 2:17am. One baby was crying. The other had just fallen asleep. I was sitting on the bathroom floor, phone in hand, watching a video about productivity."
3
Expand the Story
Slow down. Add details, thoughts, feelings. This is where your audience feels seen. Let the moment breathe.
4
Internal Conflict
Your secret weapon. Doubt, guilt, fear, confusion. This is what builds a loyal audience — people who come back because you say what they can't.
5
The Realization
Zoom out. What did this moment teach you? Not a cliché — a real insight that only your specific life could produce.
6
Connect to Them
Shift from "me" → "you." Bridge your personal story to their life. This is the pivot that turns a diary entry into content.
Example: "And if you're learning English right now, this shows up differently — but the feeling is the same…"
7
Teach
Authority mode. Structure your idea clearly. Explain, break it down, give examples. This is where the Substack earns its keep vs a Reel.
8
Actionable Part
Give them something to do: 2–3 steps, or 1 framework, or 1 exercise. Make it specific enough to start today.
9
Strong Ending + Soft CTA
Loop back to the opening scene, or drop a powerful truth. Then a soft CTA: "If this resonated, subscribe" or "Reply and tell me your story."
