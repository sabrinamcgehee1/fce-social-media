The Editing DNA (What Your 5 Inspirations All Do)
This is the formula. Every single one of your inspiration creators follows this pattern:
1. Face-to-camera talking — you're the anchor. Clean background, natural light, slightly above eye level.
2. B-roll images appearing on screen — as you mention a book, a concept, a place → a relevant image/screenshot slides in (usually from the right or bottom). This is the Giulia/Isabel/Tizi signature move.
3. Soft looping background track — lo-fi, acoustic, or ambient. Never trending audio. Same vibe every video. This creates subconscious recognition.
4. Signature font for key phrases — when you say something quotable, it appears as text on screen in your brand font (Instrument Serif italic, Sage green or white).
5. Subtle sound effects — a soft "pop" or "whoosh" when images appear. Not cartoon-loud — elegant.
6. Clean ending — either a call-to-action card or a gentle fade with your name.
Image Style for B-Roll
The images that appear while you talk are critical. Here's what to use:
Book covers — when mentioning any book (screenshot from Amazon/Goodreads, crop clean)
Screenshots — from apps, articles, your own Substack, spreadsheets you use, your phone home screen
Aesthetic stock photos — coffee, journal, laptop, city street, airport. Use Unsplash or Canva stock. Always warm-toned, light, clean.
Your own photos — twins, Brazil/US moments, your workspace, your journal. These are your moat.
Text cards — key stats, quotes, or frameworks on a Linen (#F2F0ED) background with Instrument Serif text
Never use: Busy/cluttered images, cold-toned stock, corporate photos, images with lots of text already on them

02 — Signature Series
Your "Anti-Brainrot"
A signature series is the single most powerful growth lever on Instagram/TikTok. It gives people a reason to follow (they want the next episode) and creates binge-worthy content. Here are 5 options designed for your unique angle.
★ Top Pick
The 1% Series
Tagline: "Being 1% better every day — and showing you the receipts." Each episode = one small improvement you made that week. Could be financial (saved $20 by switching X), personal (started waking at 6am), learning (new French phrase that changed how you think), parenting (bilingual trick that worked). Format: you talking to camera, showing the actual thing you changed, with the result.
Why this wins: It's your existing catchphrase turned into a content engine. It's infinitely repeatable. Each episode is standalone but part of something larger. And no one else has your specific 1% improvements (bilingual family + immigrant + finance background).
Hook formula: "1% better, day [X]: I [did this thing] and here's what happened."
Between Two Countries
Cultural observations only someone living between Brazil and the US can make. Not "differences between Brazil and America" (overdone). Instead: the specific, micro moments. "In Brazil we say 'saudade' and there's no English word for it — but I found 3 things that come close." Format: cinematic B-roll of both countries, your voiceover, text on screen.
Why it works: Unique angle, highly shareable, emotional. Americans love learning about other cultures when it's delivered through personal story, not lecture.
Money Diaries: Immigrant Edition
Real numbers. Real decisions. How you manage money across two currencies, two tax systems, two family expectations. Each episode = one specific financial move or decision you made, with context. "This month I sent R$2,000 to Brazil for [reason]. Here's how I decided the timing based on the exchange rate."
Why it works: Personal finance + immigration is an untapped niche. Most finance creators are American-born Americans. You bring a perspective nobody else has. Plus your accounting background gives quiet authority.
Building in Public
Document building Fluency Connect, growing your Substack, learning French — all at once. The chaos of being a mom of twins running multiple projects. Raw, real, Nana Del Rey style. "Week 12: Our app has 47 users. Here's what went wrong and what I'd change."
Why it works: Nana's entire brand is built on this. You have even more material — you're building a tech company, growing a newsletter, learning a third language, AND raising twins. The content writes itself.
The Anti-Comfort Zone
Each episode = you doing something uncomfortable on camera and sharing what you learned. Cold-calling a potential client. Recording a video in French (badly). Asking Michael a hard question about money. The vulnerability IS the content. Think Tizi's Anti-Brainrot but applied to personal growth through discomfort.
Why it works: Raw, real, addictive to watch. Creates emotional investment — people root for you.
My recommendation
Start with The 1% Series as your main series. It's the easiest to produce (you're already living it), the most flexible (any topic works), and it directly ties to your catchphrase. Use "Between Two Countries" as an occasional secondary format when you have a cultural observation worth sharing. Save the others for when you've established your rhythm — month 2 or 3.

07 — CapCut Workflow
Your Editing System
Starting from zero. Here's how to build the Tizi/Giulia/Isabel editing style in CapCut — step by step. Do this once, then every future video follows the same template.
Set Up Your Color Preset
Open CapCut → New Project → Import any clip → Adjust → Exposure: +5, Color Temp: +3 (slightly warm), Saturation: -8 (desaturated, clean look), Highlights: +5, Shadows: +3, Fade: +10 (slight matte finish). Save as "Sabrina Clean" preset. This gives you the soft, elevated look your inspiration creators have.
Choose Your Background Track
CapCut Audio Library → search "lo-fi study" or "soft ambient" or "acoustic morning." Pick ONE track you love. This becomes YOUR sound. Tizi uses the same vibe in every video. Set volume to 15–20% — it should be felt, not heard. The track plays from start to finish, under your voice.
Build the B-Roll Image System
Before filming, prepare 3–5 images per video (screenshots, book covers, aesthetic photos). Import them into CapCut as overlays. For each image: (1) Set size to about 35–40% of screen, (2) Position in upper right or lower left, (3) Add a "Slide In" animation (0.3s from right), (4) Add a subtle drop shadow, (5) Duration: 2–4 seconds per image. Add a soft "pop" sound effect from CapCut's SFX library when each image appears.
Set Up Caption Styles
Regular subtitles: Auto-captions → font closest to Sora or clean sans-serif → white text, black background at 30% opacity, centered bottom third. Key phrase emphasis: When you say something quotable, add a separate text element: Instrument Serif style (or elegant serif available in CapCut), larger size (22–26px), centered, white text with subtle sage green underline or background. Duration matches the spoken phrase.
Create Your End Card
In Canva, design a 1080×1920 end card: Linen (#F2F0ED) background, centered "follow for more" in Instrument Serif, your @handle in Sora, and a small sage green line underneath. Export as PNG. In CapCut, add as the final 1.5 seconds with a 0.3s fade transition. Reuse on every video.
Save as Master Template
Once steps 1–5 are set up in a project: save this project as "Sabrina Master." For each new video, duplicate this project → swap the main clip → update B-roll images → update key phrases → export. Your editing time per video drops from 45+ minutes to 15–20 minutes.
08 — Brand Rules
Do This, Never That
Print this out. Tape it to your wall. These rules keep you consistent when you're tired, rushed, or tempted to break brand.
Always Do
Use Instrument Serif for titles/headlines
Use Sora for body text and captions
Apply "Sabrina Clean" color preset to every video
Include 3–5 B-roll images per video
Use the same background track every time
Keep your feed light/clean (white space dominant)
Use sage green (#7A8F7A) as your signature accent
End every video with the same end card
Add a freebie CTA in your caption ("link in bio")
Post in English only on Sabrina EN
Never Do
Use Fluency Connect colors (navy, blue, red)
Use Nexa or any font besides Instrument Serif + Sora
Use cold/blue-toned filters
Use CapCut's default neon/colorful caption styles
Post without B-roll images (talking head alone = boring)
Use trending audio (your background track is your brand)
Mix Portuguese content into Sabrina EN
Use random Canva templates that don't match
Skip the end card to "save time"
Post without a custom cover/thumbnail
The 3-Second Recognition Test
Before posting, ask: "If someone saw this with no username — would they know it's me?" Check: Is the filter clean/warm? Is there B-roll? Is the font Instrument Serif? Is there sage green somewhere? Is there soft background music? If any of these are missing, fix before posting. Consistency builds brand. Brand builds trust. Trust builds income.
