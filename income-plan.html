The Revenue-First Rule
Every day, ask: "Did I do something today that will generate money this week?" If the answer is no, reprioritize. Content is important but content without a product behind it is a hobby. Teaching a class generates R$75-150/hour right now. Posting a Reel generates R$0 right now. Both matter — but when you're at R$2,000/month, revenue activities come first.
Sabrina's 4-Account System
The only way this works:

1. Batch filming day (1×/week, 2-3 hours): Film all 4 Reels for the week in one session. Multiple outfits, same location. Each topic filmed from FC angle + personal angle.

2. Repurpose, don't recreate: Every FC Pillar 2 video goes on Sabrina's account AND Fluency Connect. Every couple/family video goes on Sabrina + Michael's accounts. One video, multiple posts with different captions.

3. Schedule everything: Use Meta Business Suite (free) for Instagram, and TikTok's built-in scheduler. Upload all videos on batch day. They post automatically all week.

4. Carousels from scripts: The scripts already exist. Turn them into Canva carousels. 30 min per carousel max.

5. Stories are scraps: Behind-the-scenes clips, polls, reposts of your own Reels. 5-10 min/day max.

Group Class Retention Fix
Your groups failed before because of open-ended commitment + price sensitivity + schedule conflicts. Here's the fix:

1. 8-week cycles, not monthly. "Turma de 8 semanas" feels like a course with a finish line, not an endless subscription.

2. Prepaid with discount. R$150/month if paid monthly. R$320 upfront for the full 8 weeks (saves R$80). Prepaid = committed.

3. WhatsApp group accountability. Daily micro-challenge in the group chat. Students who engage in the group stay longer.

4. Fixed schedule, no exceptions. "Tuesday 7PM" — if you miss it, there's no makeup class. This filters for serious students.

5. Maximum 6 students per group. Small enough to feel personal. Big enough to be profitable.
